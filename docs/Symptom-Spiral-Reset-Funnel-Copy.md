## 0. Read this before you build

**What we're optimizing for, and how.** Cold + impulse + mobile means: comprehension in under 10 seconds, the lowest-commitment first action possible (a quiz, not a "buy"), curiosity as the engine, micro-commitments that build investment, social proof early, one sticky CTA per screen, wallet-pay at checkout, and zero forced signup before value. The quiz funnel is doing the selling — the landing page's only job is to start the quiz, and the price isn't shown until *after* the assessment delivers value.

**How this validates demand without building all 42 days (the honest way).** You sell the full program, deliver **Week 1 immediately on purchase, and unlock one day at a time.** Because a buyer only needs Day 8 on day 8, you have a full week of runway to finish Weeks 2–6 while real customers are already using Week 1. That makes the validation *real* (people pay and actually use the product), not a vaporware smoke test. If you'd rather run a pure presell with a start date a couple of weeks out, that's also fine — the one rule is: **if you take money, you deliver, and if you decide not to proceed, you refund everyone.** Don't sell something you don't intend to build.

**Meta compliance guardrails every screen obeys.** Meta's Personal Attributes policy bans ad/funnel copy that asserts or implies the viewer *has* a health or mental-health condition, and it reviews the landing page too — not just the ad. So across the whole funnel: use **first-person and general framing, never second-person diagnosis** ("I used to Google every symptom" ✅ / "Do you suffer from health anxiety?" ❌). No fear-based or "negative self-perception" messaging. No medical or cure claims. Keep a visible privacy link and the "not medical advice" line. The *assessment* lives on your own domain (post-click), which is the correct place for it — but the page must still read as supportive and educational, or it triggers retroactive rejection.

**Social proof honesty (important if you're pre-launch).** Do **not** invent user counts or reviews — fabricated testimonials are illegal (FTC), Meta-rejectable, and corrosive to trust. Every social-proof slot below is marked `[REAL NUMBERS ONLY]` with an honest pre-launch alternative you can use until you have the real thing (founder story, the science, "founding cohort / be one of the first," beta testimonials if you have any).

**The through-line that makes this convert *and* stay honest:** the assessment isn't just a lead magnet — it produces the user's **Day-0 Spiral Score**, the same number the program tracks downward over 42 days. Buying isn't "purchasing an app," it's "starting to move this number." That's the emotional hook and it's completely truthful.

## 2. THE 2-MINUTE SYMPTOM SPIRAL ASSESSMENT

> Tap-only (no typing), one question per screen, a progress bar, ~10 scored questions = ~2 minutes. Mobile thumb-zone buttons. A back button. The format itself is a series of micro-commitments that increase investment before the offer.

### Intro screen
**Heading:** Your Symptom Spiral Assessment
**Body:** 10 quick questions. About 2 minutes. There are no right answers — this is a mirror, not a test. It'll show you the shape of your loop and where it's strongest.
**Disclaimer (small):** This is a self-reflection tool, not a medical diagnosis.
**Button:** Begin →

### The questions
*(Default answer scale = tap one: **Never (0) · Rarely (1) · Sometimes (2) · Often (3) · Almost constantly (4)**, unless noted.)*

**Q1.** When you notice a new sensation in your body, how often do you search it online?
**Q2.** How often do you check your body for signs that something's wrong (pulse, lumps, skin, breathing, swallowing)?
**Q3.** How often do you ask other people — family, partner, doctors, forums — whether a symptom seems serious?
**Q4.** How often do you re-check or re-search something you've already checked?
**Q5.** When a doctor or a test result tells you you're fine, how long does the relief last?
 *(Scale: Months (0) · Weeks (1) · A few days (2) · Hours (3) · Minutes (4))*
**Q6.** How often do health worries interrupt your sleep?
**Q7.** On a typical day, how much time goes to health worry?
 *(Scale: Almost none (0) · Under 15 min (1) · 15–60 min (2) · 1–3 hours (3) · Over 3 hours (4))*
**Q8.** How often does health anxiety stop you doing things you'd otherwise do (exercise, travel, plans, intimacy)?
**Q9.** How often do you feel you need to be *certain* nothing is wrong before you can relax?
**Q10.** How often do ordinary bodily sensations make you fear a serious illness?

**Context question (not scored, used to personalize results):**
**Q11.** How long has this been part of your life?
 *(Less than 6 months · 6 months–2 years · 2–5 years · 5+ years)*

**Transition micro-screens (sprinkle 1–2 between questions to build momentum, optional):**
> "You're not alone in this — the pattern you're describing is one of the most common and most treatable anxiety patterns there is. A few more questions."

## 3. SCORING ALGORITHM

**Total Spiral Score (Day-0 baseline):**
- Sum the 10 scored questions (Q1–Q10), each 0–4 → raw 0–40.
- Normalize to 0–100: `Spiral Score = raw × 2.5`.

**Bands (named to describe the *pattern*, never to diagnose or alarm):**
| Score | Band | One-line meaning |
|-------|------|------------------|
| 0–25 | **Occasional Loop** | The pattern is present but intermittent. |
| 26–50 | **Active Loop** | The loop runs regularly and is costing you time and calm. |
| 51–75 | **Strong Loop** | A well-grooved cycle that's shaping daily decisions. |
| 76–100 | **Intense Loop** | A demanding, frequent cycle — and one that responds especially well to structured retraining. |

**Sub-scores (for personalization — show the user their dominant driver):**
- **Searching driver** = Q1 + Q4 (+ Q10 weighted ×0.5)
- **Checking driver** = Q2
- **Reassurance driver** = Q3 + Q5
- **Life-impact** = Q6 + Q7 + Q8
- **Uncertainty intolerance** = Q9
Whichever of *Searching / Checking / Reassurance* is highest = the user's **"strongest driver,"** named on the results page.

**Two design rules baked into scoring (ethical + clinical + conversion all point the same way):**
1. **High scores are framed as treatable, never as danger.** A high "Intense Loop" result must read as *"this is exactly the pattern this program is built for,"* not *"you should be worried."* Amplifying fear is unethical, clinically counterproductive (it feeds the loop), *and* it spikes refunds. Hope converts; fear churns.
2. **A safety branch on high scores.** If Spiral Score ≥ 76 **or** Q6/Q7/Q8 are maxed, the results page surfaces a gentle line recommending a licensed therapist alongside the program, plus the crisis note. Build it in.

## 9. MONEY-BACK GUARANTEE STRUCTURE

**Recommended for a validation launch: 30-day, no-questions-asked.**
> **The "Break the Loop or It's Free" Guarantee.** Use the program for up to 30 days. If it's not helping you — for any reason or no reason — email us within 30 days of purchase and we'll refund you in full. No forms, no hoops, no "prove you did the work."

**Why no-questions (not completion-conditional) for *validation* specifically:** you're trying to learn true demand. A clean, frictionless guarantee both maximizes conversion (removing all purchase risk) *and* gives you the truest signal — and the refunds themselves are data (high refunds = the offer or product isn't landing; low refunds = real product-market fit). A completion-linked guarantee ("finish 14 days first") lowers refund cost but muddies the validation signal and adds friction. Save it for after launch if refund rate becomes a problem.

**Make it honest and easy (this is the whole point):**
- Refunds processed within a few business days, stated plainly.
- One email to claim — no obstacle course. A guarantee you make hard to use is a dark pattern and it backfires in chargebacks and reviews.
- State it identically on the purchase page, checkout, and thank-you page.

**What it is NOT:** never a guarantee of a medical outcome or a "cure." It's a guarantee on the *program experience* ("if it's not for you, your money back"), not on your health. That distinction keeps you clear of FTC/Meta health-claim rules.
